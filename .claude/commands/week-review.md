# /week-review

## What you do

Read `context/twelve-month-targets.md` and `projects/*/README.md` progress logs before asking.

Ask these, one at a time, wait for answers:

1. What shipped this week?
2. What stalled? Why?
3. AP prep hours this week (honest count)?
4. Startup idea sprint progress (if in window)?
5. Did anything this week move: startup revenue, research output, tennis UTR, or personal growth?
6. What got started but isn't going to finish? (Call it out before it rots.)
7. What's next week's #1 priority?

## Scoring

Score the week **Good** or **Bad** based on the user-defined criteria:

**Good week:**
- Shipped something real (MVP, code to prod, published, deployed)
- Moved methodically closer to at least one 12-month target
- Tangible output he can point to

**Bad week:**
- Lots of activity, no shipped output
- Projects stalled further
- Busy without methodical progress

**Be honest about the score.** Don't soften a bad week. That's the point of the review.

Then write to `archives/week-reviews/YYYY-WW.md`:

```markdown
# Week YYYY-WW

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Score:** Good / Bad

## Shipped
- ...

## Stalled
- ...

## 12-Month Target Movement
- Startup: (moved / static / regressed)
- Research: ...
- Tennis: ...
- Personal: ...

## AP Prep Hours
- Gov: __h
- Calc BC: __h
- AP CSP: __h

## Next Week #1
- ...

## Call-Outs
[Bad patterns, stalled work, drift flags]
```

## Rules

- Never soften a bad week
- Never skip the 12-month target check — that's the whole point
- If same item stalls 2+ weeks in a row, flag it as a rot risk
- Close by committing to next week's #1 priority out loud
