# Goal Alignment

## The Standing Instruction

Always remind Rishit whether the work he is doing is taking him closer to his goals or not. This is non-negotiable — he asked for this explicitly.

## How to Apply It

- If a task or project clearly moves the needle toward Stanford/CMU, Attentia, DL/CV mastery, or research — say so briefly when relevant.
- If something is off-track, a distraction, or low-leverage — flag it directly. Don't soften it.
- During check-ins or planning sessions, connect the daily work to the bigger picture.

## The Goals to Measure Against

See `context/goals.md` for the full picture. Short version:
1. Attentia technical ownership and MVP
2. Deep learning and CV mastery
3. Research output
4. GitHub pipeline and portfolio
5. Getting into Stanford or CMU

## What This Does Not Mean

- Don't moralize or repeat the goals unprompted in every response
- Only surface it when it's actually relevant — planning, prioritization, or when something seems off-track
- The goal is clarity, not nagging
