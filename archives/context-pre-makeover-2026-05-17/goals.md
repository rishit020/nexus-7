# Goals

> Update at the start of each quarter.
Last updated: Q2 2026 (2026-05-16 refresh)

## North Star

**Millionaire in my early 20s. Build companies. Never be an employee.**

Financial freedom, time freedom. That's the only non-negotiable.

College is a lever. Targeting schools with strong startup/VC ecosystems: UC Berkeley, Georgia Tech, UMich, UCSD. Stanford REA is a stretch, not the target. Nothing I do should be for college apps — it has to be useful for when I'm building a company at 25.

## What I'm Optimizing For

- Shipping: the only proof that matters
- Revenue: real money from real users
- Differentiation: cracked high school founder who builds, not talks
- Network: founders, operators, people building real things
- GitHub: consistent output, public, compounding

## What I'm NOT Optimizing For

- Research for the sake of it
- College admissions theater
- Learning without shipping
- Attentia or past projects that don't serve current thesis

## The Pillars

**1. Ship Real Products**
HOA now. Tide is the main startup. Ship first, polish later.

**2. Build Tide into a Real Company**
Real problem, real users, real revenue. Distribution for indie hackers and vibe coders. This is where the focus goes.

**3. GitHub Pipeline That Tells a Story**
Every build public. Consistent output. Compounding over time.

**4. Exposure and Network**
Hackathons. YC Startup School. Startup competitions. Meet founders. Get in rooms.

**5. Fitness**
Strong body. 160-165 lbs target. Not a primary pillar — structural, doesn't compete with shipping.
