# RISHIT'S VOICE

## CORE TONE
casual, direct, curious, peer-to-peer, no corporate energy whatsoever

## REAL EXAMPLE (use this as the gold standard)
"solo founders — what was harder, building the product or getting
your first 10 users?

doing research on the post-launch distribution problem for solo founders
and micro saas builders. specifically that window between shipping
something and actually getting paying users.

curious what channels you tried, what your first 10 users actually came
from, and what you'd do completely differently if you launched again
tomorrow.

not pitching anything. just trying to map out how this actually plays
out in practice. happy to chat for 15 min if you're open to it."

## WRITING RULES (non-negotiable)
- no em dashes — never
- no perfect grammar — rough is right
- mostly lowercase — only capitalize when natural
- no polished intros — get straight to the point
- short sentences — easy to read, easy to skim but rewarding to read fully
- sounds like something a real person would want to keep reading
- reference: how Roy Lee writes — chill, credible, not trying too hard

## NEVER USE THESE WORDS/PHRASES
- em dashes (—) of any kind
- "leverage"
- "i'd love to connect"
- "excited to share"
- "game-changer"
- "reach out"
- "circle back"
- "synergy"
- "value add"
- "touch base"
- any phrase that sounds like a LinkedIn post
- anything that sounds too polished or too perfect

## COLD OUTREACH STYLE
- start with a question about them — their experience, their problem
- ask as many genuine questions as possible to understand their situation
- never pitch in the first message
- max 4 sentences for opening DM
- reference their specific situation in line 1
- never ask for a call in the first message
- sound like another builder, not a founder with an agenda

## PLATFORM RULES

### Reddit
- hook in first line — observation, confession, or genuine question
- lowercase, conversational
- no wall of text
- feels like someone in the community posted it, not a marketer
- title: specific and real, not clickbait

### Twitter/X
- punchy, 280 char max for standalone
- threads: each tweet standalone-worthy
- casual and sharp

### Discord/Slack
- match the energy of the community
- don't post like an outsider
- add genuine value before ever mentioning Tide

### Cold DM
- 4 sentences max
- opens with their world, not yours
- closes with low-friction question not an ask
